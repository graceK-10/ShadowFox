Cultivation

Propagation

This stage focuses on getting the plants ready for growth. Growers can either start with feminized seeds (guaranteed female plants) or take cuttings (clones) from mature, high-quality mother plants. Seeds are germinated (brought to life) under controlled conditions, while clones are dipped in rooting hormones to grow roots.

Key Activities: Seed germination, Rooting development for clones, Early vegetative growth for both seeds and clones.

Vegetative Growth

Once the plants have a healthy root system and a few sets of leaves, they enter the vegetative stage. Here, growers provide optimal light, temperature, and nutrients to promote strong, bushy plants. This stage can be adjusted to control plant size before they shift to flower production.

Key Activities: Providing optimal light, temperature and nutrients. Encouraging plant growth & Monitoring plant health for any diseases or nutrient deficiencies.

Flowering

When it's time for bud production, growers switch the light cycle to simulate shorter days (typically 12 hours of light and 12 hours of darkness). This triggers the plants to flower, producing the resinous buds that hold the medicinal properties. This stage requires close monitoring to ensure optimal bud development and trichome growth.

Key Activities: Switching light cycle to include flowering. Monitoring bud development to determine harvest time.

Harvesting & Curing

The buds are carefully trimmed, dried under controlled temperature and humidity to preserve quality, and then cured for several weeks to further enhance flavour and potency. After curing, the cannabis is packaged and undergoes quality testing before reaching medical dispensaries.

Key Activities: Bud trimming. Controlled drying in a cool, dark space with proper ventilation. Curing for several weeks. Packaging and quality testing
